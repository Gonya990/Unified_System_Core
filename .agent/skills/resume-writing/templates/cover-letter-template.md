# Cover Letter Tips

## DO:
- Keep to one page (3-4 paragraphs)
- Address to a specific person when possible
- Include 2-3 quantified achievements
- Show you've researched the company
- Match tone to company culture
- Use keywords from job posting

## DON'T:
- Repeat your resume word-for-word
- Use generic openings ("To Whom It May Concern")
- Focus only on what you want (focus on value you bring)
- Include salary expectations unless asked
- Use excessive jargon or buzzwords
- Send the same letter to every company

## When to Write One:
- Job posting explicitly requests it
- Highly competitive position
- You have context that doesn't fit resume (career change, gap explanation)
- Personal connection to company
- Applying to small/medium companies where it will be read

## When to Skip:
- High-volume applications through ATS
- Job posting says "no cover letter"
- Applying to large tech companies known to ignore them
- Time is severely limited (quality > quantity, but quantity matters)

# Email Application Body

When applying via email, the email body is NOT your cover letter.

## Email Body Template:
```
Subject: [Job Title] Application - [Your Name]

Dear [Hiring Manager/Recruiting Team],

Please find attached my resume and cover letter for the [Job Title]
position at [Company Name].

I am a [Your Title/Background] with [X years] of experience in
[relevant field], and I am excited about the opportunity to
contribute to [something specific about company/role].

I would welcome the chance to discuss my qualifications further.
Thank you for your consideration.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone Number]
[LinkedIn URL]

Attachments:
- [FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf]
- [FirstName_LastName_CoverLetter.pdf]
```
